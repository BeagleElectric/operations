A Message From Ownership
Why This Manual Exists

Beagle Electric is a small company doing real work in central Alberta. Every job we take on, every hour we put in, and every part we order either makes us money or costs us money. The difference between a profitable company and one that struggles isn't talent — it's process. It's whether the right information gets to the right person at the right time, every single day.

This manual exists because we want everyone on the team to understand exactly how we operate — not just what their job is, but how their piece connects to everyone else's. When Ford sends the morning report and Darryl confirms his parts order and Cole responds to the daily check-in and the crew updates their materials in Jobber — all of that adds up to clean numbers, accurate invoices, and a business that can pay good wages and reward the people who show up.

These aren't rules for the sake of rules. Every process in this manual exists because skipping it costs us money. A missing receipt means we can't bill that material. A quote that sits for two weeks means someone else got the job. Hours logged to the wrong customer mean we can't see if a job was profitable. Small gaps, repeated daily, turn into thousands of dollars of lost revenue over a year.

We want this business to grow. We want to keep good people, pay fair wages, and run bonuses that actually mean something. That only happens when the whole team follows the system — together.

How We Stay Profitable — The Basics
01
Every Hour Gets Billed to the Right Job
Unbillable time costs us money. When hours are tracked accurately, we know exactly what each job costs and whether we priced it right.
02
Every Part Gets Tied to a Job
Materials we buy but can't bill disappear from our margin. Customer name on every order. Non-ECOL receipts into Dext or Jobber notes. No exceptions.
03
Quotes Go Out Fast
The company that quotes fastest usually gets the job. A quote sitting in someone's head is revenue we're leaving on the table for a competitor.
04
Invoices Go Out on Time
We do the work, we send the invoice. Progress billing on the 20th every month. Cash flow keeps the lights on and the crew paid.
05
Communication Keeps the Schedule Moving
Ford can only dispatch accurately with good information from the field. A two-sentence WhatsApp message from you keeps the whole day running right.
06
Clean Numbers = Real Decisions
Greg needs accurate monthly numbers to make good calls on pricing, hiring, and growth. Every process in this manual feeds that data.
$
The Bonus — How It Works & Why It Matters
Every team member — Greg, Cole, Darryl, Wyatt, Dylan, and Ford — participates in a quarterly profit-sharing bonus. Bonuses are paid on profit above a 10% margin threshold. The better the business performs, the bigger the pool. When everyone follows the process, the numbers are clean, the invoices go out, and the profit is real. That's what gets shared. The system in this manual is directly connected to what lands in your pocket at the end of each quarter.

Quoting Process
From request to sent Cole & Darryl
Every quote request gets logged, assigned, assessed, and sent. Two numbers is all it takes — hours needed and parts needed. Don't overcomplicate it. Speed wins jobs.
Non-Negotiable #1
Every Request Gets Into Jobber — Same Day It Comes In
Phone, email, Facebook, word of mouth — doesn't matter. Ford logs it in Jobber as a quote request the day it arrives. Nothing sits in someone's head.
Non-Negotiable #2
Every Quote Out the Door Within One Week
One week is the hard maximum. Target is two days. A quote sitting unsent is a job we're not getting.

Quarterly Bonus Program
Greg, Cole, Darryl, Wyatt, Dylan & Ford Full Team
Every team member participates in the bonus pool. The business earns its keep first — the team shares in everything above the 10% EBDT threshold. Follow the process, keep the numbers clean, and the bonus is real.
Who is Eligible
Full Team — Greg, Cole, Darryl, Wyatt, Dylan & Ford
Every active team member participates. The business earns its keep first — the team shares in everything above the threshold. Higher earners who carry more responsibility receive a proportionally larger share.
Ford's Condition
All Three Monthly Profit Plan Reports — On Time
Ford must deliver all three Profit Plan Reports on time during the quarter to receive his bonus share. Missing even one forfeits Ford's portion for that quarter. Other crew bonuses are not affected by Ford's reporting performance.
How the Bonus is Calculated
Bonuses are calculated on profit above a 10% EBDT margin threshold for the quarter. Distribution is wage-weighted — each team member's share is proportional to their wages earned in the quarter relative to total wages paid. The bonus pool percentage is set by Greg based on quarterly results.

Example: Quarterly revenue $300,000 → 10% threshold = $30,000 → Actual EBDT $45,000 → Bonus pool calculated on the $15,000 above threshold.
